Tamagoyaki

Description

To create a delicately sweet tamagoyaki with a vibrant yellow color, I seasoned it with light soy sauce and salt.

Ingredients

  • 3 eggs (medium size)
  • (A) 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • (A) 1 teaspoon of light soy sauce
  • (A) 2 pinches of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of salad oil
  • Perilla leaves for garnish

Steps

  1. Crack the eggs into a bowl, beat them well with chopsticks to break up the whites, and mix in (A).
  2. Heat a tamagoyaki pan over medium heat, add the salad oil, and wipe off any excess oil with a paper towel.
  3. Pour one-quarter of the mixture from step 1 into the pan over medium heat. When it's half-cooked, roll it from the back to the front, then move it to the back. Repeat this process three times, and when cooked through, remove from the heat.
  4. Place step 3 onto a bamboo sushi mat and shape it. Cut it into bite-sized pieces, arrange them on a plate lined with perilla leaves, and it's ready to serve.
